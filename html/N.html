Transformation Rules
pronoms   m n
verbs     f g h
sentences M N Q R

    [f n] ~ [g n]
    -------------
    (f N) ~ (g N)

  [m f n] ~ [m g n]
  -----------------
  (M f N) ~ (M g N)

        M ~ N
    -------------
    (f M) ~ (f N)

        M ~ N
        Q ~ R
  -----------------
  (M f N) ~ (Q f R)

        N ~ Q
        N ~ R
        -----
        Q ~ R

  [f 1+n] ~ [n g (f n)]
  ---------------------
    [f n] ~ [g^n (f 0)]

where

  [g^0 N] ~ [N]
[g^1+n N] ~ [n g g^n N]  

    [m+0] ~ [m]
  [m+1+n] ~ [1+m+n]

    [0-1] ~ [0]
[(1+n)-1] ~ [n]

    [m-0] ~ [m]
  [m-1+n] ~ [(m-n)-1]

    [m*0] ~ [0]
  [m*1+n] ~ [m+m*n]

Full Parenthesis Notation
As with classical algebra, full parenthesis notation is hardly used by humans.
It is included here for the sake of completeness and exactness.
It is also included to show how removing space practically eliminates human 
readability.

        (f N) ~ (f(N))
      (f g N) ~ (f(g(N)))
    (f g h N) ~ (f(g(h(N))))

      ((f) N) ~ (f(N))
    ((f g) N) ~ (f(g(N)))
  ((f g h) N) ~ (f(g(h(N))))

      (M f N) ~ ((M)f(N))
    (M f g N) ~ (f((M)g(N)))
  (M f g h N) ~ (((M)f(N))g((M)h(N)))

    (M (f) N) ~ ((M)f(N))
  (M (f g) N) ~ (f((M)g(N)))
(M (f g h) N) ~ ((((M)f(N))g((M)h(N)))
