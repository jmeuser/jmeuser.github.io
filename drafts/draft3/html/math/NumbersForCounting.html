Counting

What do we do when we count?
Counting is not labeling; numbers are not labels.
  1. stock of number labels
  2. match a label with an item
  3. repeat 2 until there is not an item left to label
The number of items labeled is not the last label matched to the last unlabeled item.
The following demonstrates that the number of a collection of items can not be the last label matched to the last unlabeled item of the collection.

The last label assigned to the second row is 7 and the last label assigned to the third row is 1. These are not the same label. Assuming that the number of items in a collection is not altered by the method of labeling and matching, then the number of items in a collection can not be the last label matched to the last unlabeled item as witnessed by the previous example.
To label is a special form of naming.
Why chose these labels? Are the signs 1,2,3,...,9 special in some way? Would not signs such as |, ||, |||, etc be just as good?
Counting is not keeping and using labels in a definite order.
The simplest example of such labels are the conventional letters in your native language's alphabet since they are usually learned in a specific order e.g. a,b,c,d,e,f,g,h,...,x,y,z.
